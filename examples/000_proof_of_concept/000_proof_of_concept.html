Sine Wave Generation

Sine waves are the fundamental building blocks of computer music. Fourier theory states that any complex signal can be expressed as a sum of a series of simple sine waves. This simple concept allows us to represent the sound of an entire orchestra in a single wave form. There are a number of ways to generate sine waves, each with trade-offs in quality and efficiency. Which one you choose depends on the needs of your software.

Direct Sine Wave Generation

The mathematical formula for expressing a sine wave as a function of time is: y(t) = A\sin(2 \pi f t + \varphi) = A\sin(\omega t + \varphi). This formula can be used to directly render a sine wave to an audio buffer.
